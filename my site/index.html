Dancing and teaching

I started competitive latin american dancing when I was around the age of seven and only stopped when I reached my 20s. Dancing is definitely one of the major aspects that defined who I am today. Not only is it one of the most beautiful arts I know, it teaches you how to take on responsibility in a team, push beyond your limits and how to work hard for something you really want, it also allowed me to see and visit many places around the world when traveling to international competitions auch as Italy, Latvia, Norway, Poland and so many more! In 2016 I also began teaching and mentoring young aspiring dancers, which was a beautiful experience – I love seeing them improve and enjoy the amazing art of dancing.

My Interests.

Chess and puzzle solving

Many say chess is boring – to me it is one of the most exciting and challenging arts to master. The way of thinking needed to succesfully conquer and win a game of chess doesn´t only help you on the board, but also in life. As Benjamin Franklin once said: “Chess teaches foresight, by having to plan ahead; vigilance, by having to keep watch over the whole chess board; caution, by having to restrain ourselves from making hasty moves; and finally, we learn from chess the greatest maxim in life – that even when everything seems to be going badly for us we should not lose heart, but always hoping for a change for the better, steadfastly continue searching for the solutions to our problems.”
